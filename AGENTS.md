# Repository Guidance For Codex

This repository is for a nonpartisan civic education and organizing website about ranked-choice voting in Melrose, Massachusetts.

## Core Guardrails

- Never claim Melrose has adopted ranked-choice voting unless that becomes verified.
- Never claim implementation is automatic.
- Treat the Massachusetts home rule petition path as a legal and civic process requiring review.
- Separate verified facts from assumptions, open questions, and legal-review placeholders.
- Do not invent citations, examples, legal language, timelines, or election-administration details.

## Engineering Direction

- Use Astro, TypeScript, Tailwind CSS, and npm.
- Keep the first phase focused on structure, placeholders, and documentation.
- Prefer accessible semantic HTML over decorative complexity.
- Keep components small and plain-language.
- Use reusable data modules for navigation, FAQs, sources, and civic pathway steps.

## Content Direction

- Write for Melrose residents who may be curious, supportive, skeptical, or undecided.
- Use civic, respectful, nonpartisan language.
- Explain terms before relying on them.
- Mark legal or procedural uncertainty clearly.
- Keep citations close to claims once real sources are added.

## Before Finishing Work

- Run `npm run build`.
- Check that placeholder routes still render.
- Confirm the disclaimer is visible.
- Confirm no invented citations were added.
- Summarize assumptions and known placeholders.
