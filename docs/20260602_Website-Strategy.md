# Website Strategy

Date: 2026-06-02

Purpose: define the strategic direction for the Melrose RCV website before final copy, visual design, or implementation work begins.

This document is planning only. It is not final website copy.

## Strategic Frame

Melrose RCV is a nonpartisan civic education and organizing website designed to build public understanding and support for bringing ranked-choice voting (RCV) to Melrose municipal elections through a Massachusetts home rule petition.

The site should help residents move from curiosity to informed action without implying that Melrose has already adopted RCV or that implementation is automatic.

## Strategic Objectives

1. Explain RCV clearly enough that a resident can describe the basic idea to a neighbor.
2. Show how single-winner RCV works without hiding implementation details.
3. Explain why Melrose might consider RCV while respecting uncertainty and skepticism.
4. Explain the Massachusetts home rule petition pathway as a legal and civic process requiring review.
5. Build trust by being sourced, nonpartisan, plain-language, and careful about claims.
6. Convert informed residents into email subscribers, volunteers, donors, question askers, and site sharers.

## Brand And Voice Strategy

The site should feel:

- Local.
- Calm.
- Neighborly.
- Accurate.
- Civic.
- Nonpartisan.
- Serious without being stiff.
- Accessible to residents who have never heard of RCV.

The site should not feel:

- Nationalized or partisan.
- Technocratic.
- Overconfident.
- Like an official City of Melrose election page.
- Like a final legal memo.
- Like a polished campaign promising outcomes before review.

## Message Pillars

### Learn Before Deciding

Primary idea:

Residents deserve clear information before deciding whether RCV belongs in Melrose.

Supports:

- Plain-language explanations.
- Practice-oriented diagrams in future work.
- FAQ and sources.

### More Choice, Carefully Explained

Primary idea:

RCV lets voters rank candidates in order of preference, but details matter.

Supports:

- RCV definition from the factual foundation.
- Distinction between single-winner instant-runoff voting and multi-winner ranked methods.
- Clear treatment of exhausted ballots and ballot marking.

### Melrose Needs A Verified Path

Primary idea:

Any Melrose proposal would need legal review, local civic process, and state authorization.

Supports:

- Massachusetts Article LXXXIX limits local election-regulation authority.
- Home rule petition path requires local and state action.
- Implementation is not automatic.

### Trust Comes From Honesty

Primary idea:

The campaign earns trust by naming open questions and citing sources.

Supports:

- Source log.
- Open questions and risks.
- Balanced treatment of benefits and concerns.

## Homepage Strategy

The homepage should be a guided civic journey, not a landing-page sales pitch.

### 1. Hero

Purpose:

- State the campaign goal and current status.
- Invite learning before asking for support.

Required content:

- Clear statement that the site is about exploring and organizing for RCV in Melrose municipal elections.
- Visible disclaimer that Melrose has not adopted RCV unless verified in the future.
- Primary CTA: "Learn how RCV works."
- Secondary CTA: "Join updates."

Avoid:

- "Melrose is adopting RCV."
- "RCV will fix Melrose elections."
- Any hero claim that lacks a source or legal review.

### 2. Trust Bar

Purpose:

- Establish nonpartisan, source-grounded posture.

Possible trust signals:

- "Nonpartisan civic education."
- "Sources linked."
- "Legal review needed."
- "Melrose-specific questions tracked."

Avoid:

- Logos or endorsements that are not verified.
- Official City of Melrose branding unless permission is granted.

### 3. What Is RCV?

Purpose:

- Give a simple first definition.

Required content:

- RCV lets voters rank candidates in order of preference.
- RCV is an umbrella term; future copy should clarify the proposed method.

CTA:

- Link to `/what-is-rcv/`.

### 4. How RCV Works

Purpose:

- Preview the single-winner instant-runoff sequence.

Required content:

- First choices are counted first.
- If no candidate meets the threshold, the lowest candidate is eliminated.
- Ballots transfer to next ranked continuing choices.
- Counting continues under the applicable rules.

CTA:

- Link to `/how-it-works/`.

### 5. Melrose-Themed Example

Purpose:

- Make the concept local without using real candidates or real contests.

Rules:

- Use fictional civic examples only.
- Do not invent past Melrose election facts.
- Do not imply current ballot design approval.

CTA:

- "See how an example ballot works."

### 6. Why RCV For Melrose?

Purpose:

- Present the case for consideration, not guaranteed outcomes.

Required content:

- Supporters argue RCV can reduce vote-splitting pressure and let residents express backup choices.
- Research on some benefits is mixed or context-dependent.
- Melrose-specific claims need source review.

CTA:

- Link to `/why-melrose/`.

### 7. Home Rule Petition Pathway

Purpose:

- Make the legal/civic process legible.

Required content:

- Local review and approval would be needed.
- State legislative authorization would likely be needed.
- Implementation details would depend on final law and election-administration review.

CTA:

- Link to `/home-rule-petition/`.

### 8. Questions Worth Asking

Purpose:

- Respect skeptical residents and show seriousness.

Topics:

- Cost.
- Equipment.
- Voter education.
- Accessibility.
- Results timing.
- Audits and recounts.
- Exhausted ballots.

CTA:

- Link to `/faq/` or `/myths-and-facts/`.

### 9. Myths And Facts Preview

Purpose:

- Address common misconceptions while not dismissing valid concerns.

Rules:

- Use "myth" only for clearly false statements.
- Use "concern" or "question" for implementation-dependent issues.

CTA:

- Link to `/myths-and-facts/`.

### 10. Where RCV Is Used Preview

Purpose:

- Show that RCV exists in real jurisdictions while avoiding stale counts.

Required content:

- Link to sources instead of hard-coding current totals unless maintained.
- Include examples such as Maine, New York City, Cambridge, Minneapolis, Boulder, Portland, and Alameda County only with jurisdiction-specific caveats.

CTA:

- Link to `/sources/` or `/what-is-rcv/`.

### 11. Join The Effort

Purpose:

- Convert informed visitors.

Primary CTA:

- Email signup.

Secondary CTAs:

- Volunteer.
- Ask a question.
- Donate.
- Share.

### 12. Footer

Purpose:

- Provide transparency and access.

Required links:

- Sources.
- FAQ.
- Contact.
- About.
- Donate.

Required note:

- This is a campaign/civic education site, not an official City of Melrose election resource.

## Success Metrics

Educational:

- Visitors reach `/what-is-rcv/` and `/how-it-works/`.
- Visitors reach FAQ and sources from concern-oriented paths.
- Visitors spend time on home rule petition content.

Conversion:

- Email signup completion.
- Volunteer interest submission.
- Question/contact submission.
- Donation click or completion once compliance language is verified.
- Share actions.

Trust:

- Source page visits.
- FAQ visits from homepage.
- Low bounce from skeptical-entry pages.

## Strategic Guardrails

- Do not claim Melrose has adopted RCV.
- Do not claim implementation is automatic.
- Do not claim RCV is legally available without state authorization.
- Do not claim a home rule petition guarantees implementation.
- Do not invent Melrose election facts.
- Do not invent donation or compliance claims.
- Do not use partisan language.
- Do not overstate benefits.
- Do not publish ballot examples as voter instructions until reviewed.
