# Conversion Strategy

Date: 2026-06-02

Purpose: define ethical, nonpartisan conversion paths for the Melrose RCV website.

This is planning only. It does not create or change form integrations.

## Conversion Philosophy

The site should ask for action only after it gives residents enough context to trust the campaign. Conversion should feel like civic participation, not pressure.

The campaign should support multiple kinds of action:

- Learning.
- Asking.
- Sharing.
- Volunteering.
- Donating.
- Following the process.

## Primary Conversion Paths

### Email Signup

Goal:

- Build a list of residents who want updates.

Audience:

- Curious residents, supporters, skeptical residents who want to follow the process.

Best placements:

- Homepage hero secondary CTA.
- Homepage join section.
- `/contact/`.
- `/faq/` after legal/process questions.
- `/home-rule-petition/` after pathway explanation.

Recommended fields:

- Email.
- First name optional.
- ZIP or ward optional only if justified.
- Interest category optional: curious, supportive, skeptical, volunteer, official/stakeholder.

Success message:

- Confirm signup and set expectations for respectful, nonpartisan updates.

Risks:

- Asking for too much too soon.
- Missing privacy expectations.
- Treating signup as endorsement.

Governance:

- Do not imply email signup means support for RCV.
- Add privacy policy before launch.

### Volunteer Interest

Goal:

- Identify residents willing to help with civic education and organizing.

Audience:

- Civic-minded supporters.

Best placements:

- Homepage join section.
- `/contact/`.
- `/about/`.
- `/why-melrose/`.

Recommended fields:

- Name.
- Email.
- City/neighborhood optional.
- Ways to help.
- Notes/questions.

Volunteer categories:

- Talk with neighbors.
- Help at events.
- Research/source review.
- Plain-language review.
- Accessibility review.
- Outreach.
- Donation/compliance support after review.

Risks:

- Making volunteering feel like partisan campaigning.
- Collecting unnecessary personal data.
- Offering roles the campaign cannot support.

Governance:

- Use nonpartisan language.
- Do not ask volunteers to repeat unverified claims.

### Contact Or Question Submission

Goal:

- Give residents a way to ask questions, flag concerns, or suggest corrections.

Audience:

- Skeptical residents, source checkers, officials, journalists, residents with practical questions.

Best placements:

- `/contact/`.
- `/faq/`.
- `/sources/`.
- `/myths-and-facts/`.
- Footer.

Recommended categories:

- General question.
- Concern about RCV.
- Source/correction.
- Volunteer.
- Media/local official inquiry.

Risks:

- No response expectation.
- Blurring official election help with campaign contact.

Governance:

- Clearly direct official voting questions to official Melrose or Massachusetts election resources.
- Do not give legal or official election-administration advice.

### Donate Or Support

Goal:

- Allow supporters to fund civic education once compliance is reviewed.

Audience:

- Supporters and donors.

Best placements:

- Header/footer.
- Homepage join section.
- `/donate/`.
- `/about/`.

Required before launch:

- Verify legal entity.
- Verify contribution rules.
- Verify reporting obligations.
- Verify donation processor.
- Add privacy/refund language if needed.

Risks:

- Invented compliance claims.
- Confusing issue advocacy, campaign finance, and municipal petition rules.
- Asking for donations before entity status is clear.

Governance:

- Donation page may remain a placeholder until compliance review.
- Provide non-monetary alternatives.

### Share With Neighbors

Goal:

- Encourage residents to spread accurate educational material.

Audience:

- Curious residents and supporters.

Best placements:

- Homepage.
- `/what-is-rcv/`.
- `/how-it-works/`.
- `/faq/`.
- `/sources/`.

Recommended share assets:

- "What is RCV?" page.
- FAQ page.
- Home rule petition page.
- Source page.

Risks:

- Share text overstates claims.
- Social snippets imply adoption.

Governance:

- Use careful default share copy.
- Do not include unsupported claims in metadata.

### Learn More Through FAQ And Sources

Goal:

- Convert uncertainty into informed engagement.

Audience:

- Skeptical residents, officials, journalists, source checkers.

Best placements:

- Every page.

Recommended links:

- "Read common questions."
- "Check sources."
- "See open questions."

Risks:

- Treating education as a secondary conversion.

Governance:

- Source and FAQ visits are trust conversions, not failures.

## CTA Hierarchy

Primary CTAs:

- Learn how RCV works.
- Join updates.
- Ask a question.

Secondary CTAs:

- Volunteer.
- Donate.
- Share.
- Check sources.

Tertiary CTAs:

- Read FAQ.
- Read home rule path.
- See Melrose election context.

## CTA Copy Rules

Use:

- "Learn how RCV works."
- "Join updates."
- "Ask a question."
- "Volunteer."
- "Support the effort."
- "Share with a neighbor."
- "Check the sources."

Avoid:

- "Adopt RCV now."
- "Make every vote count."
- "Guarantee majority winners."
- "Fix Melrose elections."
- "Stop politicians."
- Any partisan or pressure-driven CTA.

## Ethical Conversion Guardrails

- Do not count agreement as a prerequisite for asking questions.
- Do not hide uncertainty to increase conversions.
- Do not use fear-based language.
- Do not make donation asks before compliance is verified.
- Do not imply official City of Melrose endorsement.
- Do not use supporter testimonials until permission and accuracy are verified.
