# Why consider RCV in Melrose?

Melrose RCV is a nonpartisan civic education and organizing effort. The goal is to help residents understand ranked-choice voting and consider whether Melrose should pursue it for municipal elections through a Massachusetts home rule petition.

Melrose has a mayor-council form of government, and the City Council has 11 members: 4 councilors-at-large and 7 ward councilors. [source: mma-melrose] [source: melrose-charter-article-2]

Any RCV proposal would need to be designed around Melrose's actual offices, charter, election administration, and legal authority. This site should not assume which offices would be covered.

## What supporters hope RCV could improve

Supporters often point to three possible benefits.

First, RCV lets voters express more than one preference. A voter can rank a favorite candidate and backup choices in the same contest. [source: ncsl-rcv]

Second, RCV is designed to reduce some pressure around vote splitting in crowded races. Voters can support a favorite candidate while still ranking other acceptable candidates.

Third, single-winner RCV uses runoff-style rounds without requiring a separate runoff election. Whether that matters in Melrose depends on local election rules and would need legal review. [source: mit-irv]

## What we should not overpromise

RCV does not guarantee higher turnout, lower costs, more civil campaigns, better candidates, or perfect majority outcomes. Research and local experience are mixed, and many effects depend on ballot design, voter education, candidate fields, administration, and political context.

## Why local review matters

Melrose should ask practical questions before taking a position:

- Which offices would a proposal cover?
- Would single-seat and multi-seat offices need different methods?
- Would RCV replace or change any preliminary election process?
- What equipment, software, staff training, voter education, audits, recounts, and result-reporting rules would be needed?
- How would residents who are skeptical or unfamiliar with RCV be included in the conversation?
