# Sources and claim policy

This site should let residents check the basis for factual claims. Source IDs in page copy and data files connect claims back to the structured source log.

## Source hierarchy

Use official sources for legal, municipal, and election-administration facts:

- City of Melrose official pages.
- Melrose Charter and ordinances.
- Massachusetts Constitution, General Laws, General Court, and Secretary of the Commonwealth.
- Official election administrators.

Use research and civic-design sources for implementation and plain-language education:

- NCSL.
- MIT Election Lab.
- Center for Civic Design.
- Ranked Choice Voting Resource Center.
- Peer-reviewed or academic research.

Use advocacy sources carefully:

- FairVote and local advocacy sites can help explain supporter arguments.
- Advocacy sources should not stand alone for legal, cost, turnout, equipment, or Melrose-specific administrative claims.

## Claims we should not make without verification

- Melrose has adopted RCV.
- RCV will be used in the next local election.
- Local approval alone is enough.
- A home rule petition guarantees implementation.
- RCV will save money.
- Current voting machines already support RCV.
- Results will be final on election night.
- Donations are tax deductible.

## Source gaps

Before launch, the campaign should recheck current legal and legislative sources, confirm Melrose charter provisions, verify election equipment and tabulation questions, and review all donation/compliance language.
