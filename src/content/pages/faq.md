# FAQ for Melrose residents

This FAQ is written for residents who are curious, supportive, skeptical, or undecided. It should stay practical, sourced, and honest about what has not been decided.

## Basics

Ranked-choice voting lets voters rank candidates in order of preference. In a single-winner RCV election, first choices are counted first. If no candidate reaches the winning threshold, the lowest candidate is eliminated and ballots can transfer to each voter's next ranked continuing candidate. [source: ncsl-rcv] [source: mit-irv]

## Ballot marking

You generally do not have to rank every candidate in RCV examples reviewed, and you can usually vote for only one candidate. But if every candidate you rank is eliminated, your ballot may have no remaining continuing choice in later rounds. Melrose-specific public instructions would need official review. [source: boulder-rcv]

Ranking a second choice does not hurt your first choice while your first choice remains in the count. Later rankings are backups.

## Melrose status

Melrose has not adopted ranked-choice voting. This site must not claim that RCV is already approved, already being implemented, or available for the next election.

## Home rule

The research indicates Melrose likely needs state authorization through a home rule petition because Massachusetts municipalities do not have broad local authority to regulate elections. The exact path requires legal review. [source: ma-constitution]

Local approval would not make implementation automatic. The Legislature, Governor, final petition text, local requirements, and election-administration planning all matter.

## Implementation

Several questions remain open:

- Which offices would be covered?
- Would RCV replace or change preliminary elections?
- What would implementation cost?
- Would voting machines or software need to change?
- How would voters learn the system?
- How would results, audits, and recounts work?

Those answers should come from legal, municipal, and election-administration review, not campaign guesswork.

## Is this partisan?

No. Melrose RCV is framed as a nonpartisan civic education and organizing effort. The site should not use partisan language or suggest RCV is meant to help any party, faction, or candidate.
