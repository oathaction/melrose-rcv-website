# Melrose elections and RCV context

This campaign site is not an official City of Melrose election resource. Residents should use the City of Melrose Elections Office and the Massachusetts Secretary of the Commonwealth for official voter registration, polling place, vote-by-mail, candidate, ballot, and result information. [source: melrose-elections]

## Who administers Melrose elections

The City of Melrose Elections Office says it directs municipal, state, and federal elections in Melrose, conducts elections, oversees polling places, maintains voter registration records, and provides information to eligible voters and candidates. The office is located at City Hall, 562 Main Street. [source: melrose-elections]

## Municipal government context

The Massachusetts Municipal Association profile describes Melrose as having a mayor-council form of government. [source: mma-melrose]

The Melrose Charter provides for an 11-member City Council: 4 councilors-at-large and 7 ward councilors, one from each ward. Councilors serve 2-year terms. [source: melrose-charter-article-2]

The official 2025 local election results notice listed all 11 City Council seats and 3 at-large School Committee seats on the November 4, 2025 ballot. [source: melrose-2025-results]

## Details that need more review

Before public copy gets more specific, the campaign should verify current charter provisions for mayoral elections, School Committee composition and terms, preliminary elections, nomination papers, and any recent amendments. Those details should be checked against official current sources and, where needed, municipal counsel.

## RCV questions for Melrose election administration

A serious proposal would need answers to practical questions:

- What voting equipment and ballot scanners does Melrose use?
- Can current systems support ranked ballots and cast vote records?
- Who would tabulate RCV rounds?
- How would write-ins, skipped rankings, repeated rankings, overvotes, and blank rankings be handled?
- How would preliminary and certified results be reported?
- How would audits and recounts work?
- What voter education and poll-worker training would be needed?
- What would implementation cost?

No public copy should answer those questions by assumption.
